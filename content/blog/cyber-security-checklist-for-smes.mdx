Cyber attacks are no longer a big-company problem. Attackers increasingly target small and medium businesses precisely because they tend to have weaker defences. The good news: most breaches exploit basic gaps, so a handful of practical controls dramatically reduces your risk. Use this checklist as a starting point.

## The essentials

### 1. Strong authentication everywhere

Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on email, banking, and any admin account. A stolen password alone should never be enough to get in.

### 2. Keep software updated

Most attacks use known vulnerabilities that already have fixes. Turn on automatic updates for operating systems, browsers, and business apps.

### 3. Reliable, tested backups

Follow the 3-2-1 rule: three copies of your data, on two types of media, with one off-site. Then **test a restore** — a backup you can't recover from is worthless.

### 4. Email and phishing protection

Phishing is the most common entry point. Use email filtering, and train staff to spot suspicious links and payment-change requests.

### 5. Endpoint protection

Install reputable anti-malware on every device, and encrypt laptops and phones so a lost device doesn't become a data breach.

### 6. Least-privilege access

Give staff access only to what they need. Remove accounts promptly when people leave.

## Building a security culture

Technology alone isn't enough. Short, regular staff awareness sessions — how to recognise phishing, how to report incidents — are among the cheapest and most effective controls you can put in place.

## Know your response plan

Decide in advance who to call, how to isolate an affected machine, and how to communicate during an incident. A calm, rehearsed response limits the damage.
