Every growing business eventually hits the same question: do we buy an existing product, or build software tailored to how we actually work? Both can be the right answer — it depends on your processes, budget, and how much of a competitive edge the software gives you.

## When off-the-shelf makes sense

Ready-made products are fast to deploy and lower cost up front. They're a great fit when:

- Your process is standard and well-served by existing tools (accounting, payroll, basic CRM).
- You need something running quickly.
- You're happy to adapt your workflow to the software.

The trade-off is flexibility. You get what the vendor built, and you may pay for features you never use — or work around ones you need but don't have.

## When custom software wins

Custom development pays off when the software **is** your advantage, not just a utility. Consider building when:

- Your workflow is unique and existing tools force awkward compromises.
- You need deep integration with other systems you already run.
- The software is central to your customer experience or revenue.
- You want to own the roadmap rather than wait on a vendor.

Custom costs more initially, but it fits your business exactly and grows with you — no per-seat licensing surprises as you scale.

## A simple way to decide

Ask three questions:

1. **Is this a core differentiator or a commodity?** Commodity work → buy. Differentiator → build.
2. **How well do existing tools fit?** If you'd customise 80% of an off-the-shelf product, custom is often cheaper over time.
3. **What's the total cost of ownership?** Include licences, integration, and workarounds — not just the sticker price.

## The hybrid reality

Many businesses land in the middle: off-the-shelf for commodity needs, custom for the workflows that set them apart, connected with integrations. A good technology partner helps you draw that line sensibly.
