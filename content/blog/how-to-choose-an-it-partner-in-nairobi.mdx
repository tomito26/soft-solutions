Technology decisions have long-lasting consequences, and the partner you choose to guide them matters as much as the technology itself. Whether you need software built, systems secured, or infrastructure modernised, here's how to pick an IT company in Nairobi you can rely on.

## Look for range and depth

Your needs will evolve. A partner who covers [software development](/software-development), [cloud](/cloud-services), [cyber security](/cyber-security), and [networking](/networking) can support you end-to-end — instead of leaving you to coordinate several vendors who each blame the others when something breaks.

## Ask about process, not just skills

Good outcomes come from good process. Ask how a prospective partner:

- Scopes and plans work before writing a line of code.
- Communicates progress and manages change.
- Handles support and response times after go-live.

A clear, repeatable process is a strong signal of reliability.

## Check local understanding

An IT partner who knows the local context — connectivity realities, power considerations, compliance, and the way business is done in Kenya — will design solutions that actually work on the ground, not just in theory.

## Prioritise security and support

Security should be built into everything a partner does, not bolted on afterwards. And find out what happens **after** delivery: responsive, ongoing support is often what separates a good partner from a great one.

## Ask for evidence

Look for real examples of work and outcomes. [Case studies](/case-studies) and references tell you far more than a sales pitch. Ask what problem was solved, how, and what the result was.

## Trust the relationship

Finally, the best partnerships are built on communication and trust. You want a team that listens, explains things clearly, and treats your goals as their own.
