Your network is the foundation everything else runs on — cloud apps, VoIP calls, payments, backups. When it's slow or unreliable, the whole business feels it. A little planning up front avoids years of frustration. Here are the practices we build into every network we deliver.

## Start with the right foundation

### Structured cabling

Even in a wireless world, a proper wired backbone matters. Quality cabling and well-organised patching make the network faster, easier to troubleshoot, and far simpler to expand later.

### Business-grade equipment

Consumer routers aren't built for sustained business load. Business-grade switches, routers, and access points deliver better throughput, security features, and reliability.

## Design for reliability

- **Plan for redundancy.** A backup internet link and, where it matters, redundant hardware keep you online when something fails.
- **Segment your network.** Separate guest Wi-Fi, staff devices, and critical systems so a problem in one area doesn't spread.
- **Manage power.** UPS units protect equipment from the outages and surges common on local grids.

## Build security in from day one

A network is a common attack surface. At minimum:

- Use a properly configured firewall.
- Change default passwords on every device.
- Keep firmware updated.
- Use strong, separate credentials for admin access and guest Wi-Fi.

## Plan for growth and visibility

Design with headroom so adding staff or a new office doesn't mean starting over. And put monitoring in place — you can't fix what you can't see. Good monitoring flags issues before your users notice them.

## Don't forget maintenance

A network isn't "set and forget." Regular reviews, firmware updates, and capacity checks keep performance high and security tight over time.
